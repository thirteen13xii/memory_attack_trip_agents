## **📅 Daily Itinerary & Recommendations**

### **Day 1: Beijing Arrival & Imperial Grandeur**
*   **Morning (10:00 AM):** Your historical journey begins as you **arrive in Beijing**. I recommend pre-booking a private transfer to your hotel to avoid the initial hustle.
*   **Midday (12:00 PM):** Check into **The Peninsula Beijing**. Its location near Wangfujing is perfect—you're steps away from luxury shopping and historic hutongs. Take a moment to freshen up and perhaps enjoy the hotel's renowned afternoon tea later in the week.
*   **Afternoon (2:00 PM):** Visit the **Forbidden City**. *Pro Tip:* Book your tickets online well in advance via their official platform. Consider hiring a licensed guide at the entrance for about 2-3 hours to truly understand the stories behind the halls and courtyards. Wear comfortable shoes!
*   **Evening (7:00 PM):** Indulge in a quintessential Beijing experience: dinner at **Da Dong Roast Duck**. Their innovation on the classic Peking duck is legendary. Be sure to reserve a table ahead of time.

### **Day 2: Rituals, Art & Opera**
*   **Morning (9:00 AM):** Explore the **Temple of Heaven**. Come early to see locals practicing tai chi, dancing, and other activities in the surrounding park—it’s a beautiful slice of daily life.
*   **Afternoon (1:00 PM):** Embrace the lively, communal spirit of Chinese dining at **Haidilao Hot Pot**. The theatrical service (like noodle dancing) is part of the fun. Don't be shy to ask for help with the sauce station!
*   **Late Afternoon (3:00 PM):** Dive into Beijing's contemporary scene at the **798 Art District**. This converted factory complex is filled with galleries, cafes, and striking industrial architecture. It's perfect for casual exploration and photography.
*   **Evening (8:00 PM):** Experience the artistry of **Beijing Opera at Liyuan Theatre**. The elaborate costumes and makeup are mesmerizing. Shows here often include English subtitles, making the stories accessible.

### **Day 3: The Great Wall & Fine Dining**
*   **Full Day (8:00 AM):** Your **day trip to the Great Wall** is the pinnacle. I suggest visiting the **Mutianyu section**—it's less crowded than Badaling, offers cable car access, and has a thrilling toboggan ride down. Arrange a private car or join a reputable tour for the 1.5-hour journey each way. Pack water, snacks, and wear excellent walking shoes.
*   **Evening (7:00 PM):** Enjoy a sublime culinary finale at **TRB Hutong**. Dining in a restored temple is an experience in itself. This is a special-occasion restaurant, so smart casual attire is recommended.

### **Day 4: Film, Gardens & Transition**
*   **Morning (10:00 AM):** Immerse yourself in global cinema at the **Beijing International Film Festival**. Check the schedule beforehand to book tickets for specific screenings or talks that interest you.
*   **Afternoon (2:00 PM):** Find tranquility at the **Summer Palace**. Stroll around Kunming Lake, explore Longevity Hill, and admire the intricate Long Corridor. You can take a short dragon boat ride for a different perspective.
*   **Evening (6:00 PM):** Check out from The Peninsula and prepare for your next adventure. Have your luggage stored securely if your flight is early the next day.

### **Day 5: To the Ancient Capital & Terracotta Warriors**
*   **Morning (9:00 AM):** Fly from **Beijing to Xi'an**. The flight is about 2 hours. Upon arrival, head straight to your hotel (I recommend one near the city wall or within the Bell & Drum Tower area for convenience).
*   **Afternoon (11:00 AM / 3:00 PM):** Witness the awe-inspiring **Terracotta Army**. It's about an hour's drive from downtown Xi'an. A guide is highly valuable here to explain the history and significance of the different pits. Afterwards, you can explore other sites like the **Banpo Neolithic Village Museum** or return to the city to walk the **Ancient City Wall**.

### **Day 6: Xi'an's Cultural Heart**
*   **Full Day (10:00 AM):** Deepen your exploration of **Xi'an's historical sites**. Must-sees include:
    *   The **Big Wild Goose Pagoda**, a symbol of the city.
    *   The **Bell and Drum Towers** at the city center.
    *   The **Muslim Quarter**, a vibrant area for street food and culture. Try the "yangrou paomo" (crumbled bread in mutton stew).
    *   The **Shaanxi History Museum** (requires online booking in advance).

### **Day 7: Final Explorations & Departure**
*   **Morning (9:00 AM):** Use your final morning for any last-minute discoveries. Perhaps a bicycle ride on top of the **City Wall** for a unique view, or a revisit to the Muslim Quarter for souvenirs.
*   **Afternoon (2:00 PM):** Depart from **Xi'an**, concluding a profound journey through millennia of Chinese history.

## **💡 Practical Tips & Advice**

1.  **Visa & Documents:** Ensure your Chinese tourist visa (L-Visa) is secured before travel. Keep photocopies of your passport and visa separately.
2.  **Currency:** Have some Chinese Yuan (RMB) in cash for small vendors, taxis, and markets. While Alipay/WeChat Pay are ubiquitous, international cards can be used in hotels and upscale restaurants.
3.  **Connectivity:** Download a reliable VPN service *before* arriving in China to access Google, WhatsApp, Instagram, etc. Consider purchasing a local SIM card or an eSIM for data.
4.  **Transport:** Download **Didi** (China's Uber) and **Baidu Maps** or **Amap**. For the Beijing-Xi'an flight, book a morning departure to maximize your day.
5.  **Comfort:** Pack comfortable walking shoes—you'll need them. The weather can vary; check forecasts and pack layers.
6.  **Etiquette:** At temples and historical sites, dress modestly. It's polite to receive business cards or items with both hands.
