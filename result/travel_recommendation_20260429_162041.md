## Daily Itineraries

### Day 1: Beijing – Imperial Majesty & Musical Evenings  
**Theme:** Arrival & Imperial Splendor  

| Time          | Activity                                 |
|---------------|------------------------------------------|
| 10:00 AM      | Arrive at Beijing Capital Airport (PEK)  |
| 11:00 AM      | Check-in at The Peninsula Beijing        |
| 1:00 PM       | Visit the Forbidden City                 |
| 7:00 PM       | Attend Beijing Music Festival            |
| 9:00 PM       | Dinner at Da Dong Roast Duck            |

**Your Morning & Midday**  
Welcome to Beijing! After landing, a private transfer (~45–60 min) will whisk you to **The Peninsula Beijing** in Wangfujing, a luxurious hub steps from the Forbidden City. Drop your bags, freshen up, and perhaps grab a light bite at the hotel’s **Jing** restaurant or the nearby **Siji Minfu** for a quick duck pancake. At 1:00 PM, walk or take a short ride to the **Forbidden City**. Pro tip: tickets must be booked online in advance with your passport details. The sprawling complex deserves at least 3 hours – follow the central axis from the Meridian Gate to the Imperial Garden, then exit via the north gate. Climb **Jingshan Park** just across the street for a breathtaking bird’s-eye view of the golden roofs.

**Evening Elegance**  
By 7:00 PM, you’ll be seated at the **Beijing Music Festival**. Venues vary; the iconic **National Centre for the Performing Arts (NCPA)** is a likely candidate, a short ride from your hotel. Dress smart-casual and immerse yourself in world-class classical music. After the concert (around 9:00 PM), a car will take you to **Da Dong Roast Duck** (the original Dongsi branch or Jinbao Place location). Their legendary “super-skinny” roast duck is a must, alongside creative dishes like braised sea cucumber. Reservations are essential.

**Travel Advisor Tips for Day 1**
- Forbidden City ticket portal opens 7 days ahead; set an alarm!
- Wear comfortable walking shoes – you’ll easily hit 10,000 steps.
- Jet-lag drag? Sneak a 20-minute power nap before the evening show.
- Da Dong’s tasting menu can be planned in advance for a seamless experience.

### Day 2: Beijing – Great Wall Hiking & Cinematic Nights  
**Theme:** Ancient Defense Meets Modern Film  

| Time          | Activity                                                   |
|---------------|------------------------------------------------------------|
| 7:00 AM       | Full-day trip to the Great Wall (Mutianyu section)        |
| 7:00 PM       | Screening at Beijing International Film Festival          |
| 9:30 PM       | Dinner at TRB Hutong                                      |

**Rise and Climb**  
An early breakfast at the hotel fuels you for a 1.5–2-hour drive to the **Mutianyu Great Wall**. This section is beautifully restored yet less crowded than Badaling. A private car/driver is highly recommended (your hotel can arrange one for the day). Take the cable car up, then hike along the watchtowers toward the wilder, unrestored parts. For the way down, why not try the exhilarating **toboggan ride**? Plan to finish by early afternoon; grab a rustic lunch at **The Schoolhouse or Brickyard** nearby, which serve farm-to-table fare with a view.

**Evening Glamour**  
Return to the city by late afternoon to refresh. At 7:00 PM, you’ll be at a **Beijing International Film Festival** screening – perhaps at the **China Film Archive** or a downtown cinema. After the credits roll, head to **TRB Hutong**, a Michelin-starred contemporary European restaurant set in a 600-year-old temple courtyard near the Forbidden City. The degustation menu is sublime; book a table in the glass-walled atrium for a magical atmosphere.

**Travel Advisor Tips for Day 2**
- Leave at 7:00 AM sharp to avoid morning traffic and wall queues.
- Pack water, sunscreen, and a power bank; wear layers (the wall can be windy).
- Film festival tickets often sell out fast – have your hotel concierge secure them in advance.
- TRB Hutong is popular for celebrations; mention if it’s a special occasion.

### Day 3: Beijing – Temple Fairs & Xi’an – Ancient Capital Glow  
**Theme:** Festive Beijing to Poetic Xi’an  

| Time          | Activity                                                        |
|---------------|------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 9:00 AM       | Experience Spring Festival Temple Fair                          |
| 11:30 AM      | Lunch at Jing Yaa Tang                                          |
| 2:00 PM       | Fly from Beijing to Xi’an                                       |
| 4:00 PM       | Arrive at Xi’an Xianyang Airport                                 |
| 5:00 PM       | Check-in at Sofitel Legend People's Grand Hotel Xi’an            |
| 7:30 PM       | Watch *The Song of Everlasting Sorrow* at Huaqing Palace        |
| 9:30 PM       | Dinner at De Fa Chang                                           |

**A Morning of Folk Festivities**  
Today you’re soaked in the vibrant atmosphere of the **Spring Festival Temple Fair**. These fairs (often at Ditan Park or Longtan Park during Chinese New Year) brim with folk performances, acrobatics, sugar figurines, and street snacks. Arrive early (8:30 AM) to beat the crowds. Savor treats like *tanghulu* (candied hawthorn skewers) and *jianbing* while watching lion dances.  

⚠️ **A Note on Timing & Itinerary Adjustment Recommended**  
The original schedule places a sit-down lunch at 11:30 AM and a 2:00 PM flight. This is extremely tight: Jing Yaa Tang is in Sanlitun (30+ min from the temple fair, another 40+ min to the airport). **A smoother alternative:** skip the formal lunch, enjoy a feast of street food at the fair, and leave for the airport by 11:30 AM. Alternatively, if you must dine at Jing Yaa Tang, book a 10:30 AM “dim sum brunch” and depart for the airport immediately after. Your 2:00 PM domestic flight requires check-in by 1:00 PM. We strongly advise the first option.

**Touchdown in Xi’an & a Romantic Evening**  
Arrive in Xi’an at 4:00 PM. A private driver will transfer you to the grand **Sofitel Legend** (a retro-chic heritage hotel in the city center). Check in quickly, then by 6:15 PM depart for **Huaqing Palace** (Lintong, ~40 min drive) to witness the spectacular outdoor musical *The Song of Everlasting Sorrow*. Set on the actual palace grounds, this Tang Dynasty love story unfolds with fire, water, and hundreds of performers. After the show (ends ~9:00 PM), drive back to the city for a dumpling banquet at **De Fa Chang** near the Bell Tower. Their multi-course set features dumplings shaped like flowers, animals, and symbols – a fun, tasty tradition. De Fa Chang usually stays open until 10:00 PM, so a 9:30 PM arrival (or slightly later) is feasible; call ahead to confirm.

**Travel Advisor Tips for Day 3**
- Confirm flight and show tickets in advance; the Huaqing show is seasonal (usually spring–autumn).
- Keep an eye on the weather – winter evenings in Xi’an can be chilly; bring a warm coat.
- If the 9:30 PM dinner feels rushed, you could switch dinner to **First Noodle under the Sun** for a quick, iconic *biang biang* noodle experience.

### Day 4: Xi’an – Warriors, Dumplings & Dancing Fountains  
**Theme:** Qin Dynasty Might & Tang Dynasty Delight  

| Time          | Activity                                               |
|---------------|--------------------------------------------------------|
| 8:00 AM       | Visit the Terracotta Army                               |
| 1:30 PM       | Lunch at Jiasan Soup Dumplings                          |
| 7:00 PM       | Enjoy Tang Dynasty Music and Dance Show                 |
| 8:30 PM       | Dinner at Xi’an Restaurant                              |
| 9:30 PM       | Stroll through Great Tang All Day Mall Light Show       |

**Morning with the Emperor’s Army**  
After breakfast, drive to the **Terracotta Army** (again Lintong, ~1h). Arrive by 9:00 AM to beat the tour groups. Explore Pit 1, 2, and 3, plus the Bronze Chariots exhibition – a staggering testament to Qin Shi Huang’s ambition. A local guide can enrich the experience enormously. Return to Xi’an around noon.

**Muslim Quarter & Evening Extravaganza**  
Drop by **Jiasan Soup Dumplings** in the vibrant Muslim Quarter for their famous *tangbao* (soup-filled buns). Expert tip: nibble a hole, sip the broth, then enjoy. Spend the afternoon wandering the quarter’s narrow lanes, climbing the **Bell Tower**, or biking the **City Wall**.  
At 7:00 PM, be seated for the **Tang Dynasty Music and Dance Show** (often at the Tang Dynasty Palace or Shaanxi Grand Opera House). Colorful costumes and classical instruments recreate the golden age. Follow it with a hearty Shaanxi meal at **Xi’an Restaurant**, then walk over to the **Great Tang All Day Mall** near the Big Wild Goose Pagoda. The nightly light show (9:30 PM) is a modern marvel of synchronized illuminations and fountains – a perfect way to end the day.

**Travel Advisor Tips for Day 4**
- Terracotta Army tickets can be booked online; an audio guide is a good budget option.
- Jiasan is famously crowded – off-peak hours (like 1:30 PM) are smart.
- The Tang show often includes a dumpling dinner option – if you prefer to save time, you could combine the show and dinner, then try Xi’an Restaurant for lunch the next day.

**Skyline Soirée**  
A 2-hour flight whisks you to ultra-modern Shanghai. From PVG, the **Maglev train** (8 min to Longyang Rd, then taxi) is a fun intro to the city’s speed. Check into the **Peninsula on the Bund** – its rooms face the Pudong skyline and the Huangpu River. For a classic Shanghai lunch, head to **Jia Jia Tang Bao** (expect a queue, but it moves fast) for their sublime crab & pork soup dumplings. Spend the afternoon strolling the **Bund** promenade, admiring the colonial-era buildings and the futuristic skyscrapers across the river.

**Acrobatics & River Views**  
At 7:00 PM, take a taxi to **Shanghai Circus World** in Jing’an for *ERA Intersection of Time*, a breathtaking blend of Chinese acrobatics, martial arts, and multimedia effects. Post-show, circle back to the Bund for a late dinner at **M on the Bund**. The terrace views of the illuminated Pudong skyline are iconic; the modern European cuisine is equally impressive.

**Travel Advisor Tips for Day 5**
- Download Didi (China’s Uber) for seamless taxi ordering; or use the metro (line 2 connects Jing’an to the Bund).
- ERA tickets can be booked online; mid-range seats still offer great views.
- M on the Bund requires smart casual attire and a reservation for the terrace.

**Morning Reels, Afternoon Runways**  
Your morning is dedicated to the **Shanghai International Film Festival**; venues range from the Shanghai Grand Theatre to multiplexes in Xintiandi. Check the official schedule and pick a screening that captivates you. Between events, grab a light lunch at **Din Tai Fung** (legendary soup dumplings) or wander the leafy streets of the French Concession. At 2:00 PM, you’re invited to a **Shanghai Fashion Week** show – an exclusive peek at China’s cutting-edge designers. Venues often shift (Xintiandi, Jing’an temples, or industrial spaces), so plan your route accordingly.

**Home-Style Comfort**  
Evening brings comfort food at **Shanghai Grandmother** (上海姥姥), a beloved chain serving authentic Shanghainese home cooking: think braised pork belly, smoked fish, and scallion oil noodles. It’s unpretentious and deeply satisfying. After dinner, the Bund is just a stone’s throw away for a farewell stroll.

**Travel Advisor Tips for Day 6**
- Fashion Week invitations are usually by guest list; ensure your host has registered you.
- The French Concession is perfect for coffee breaks – try **Manner Coffee** or **Pain Chaud**.
- If you crave one last cultural hit, the **Shanghai Museum** or **Power Station of Art** are world-class.

## Practical Tips for a Smooth Journey

### Documentation
- **Visa**: Most travelers need a tourist (L) visa; apply well in advance. Keep a copy of your invitation letter if you have one.
- **Passport validity**: At least 6 months beyond your departure date.

### Money & Connectivity
- **Cashless society**: Almost everything is paid via **WeChat Pay or Alipay**. Set them up before departure (link an international card; some now accept foreign cards). Carry a small amount of cash for temple fairs or small vendors.
- **VPN & Internet**: Install a reliable VPN before you leave to access Google, WhatsApp, Instagram, etc. Get an eSIM or local SIM for data (China Mobile, Unicom).
- **Language**: Download Pleco (dictionary) and a translation app. Learning “Ni hao” and “Xie xie” goes a long way.

### Getting Around
- **Didi** (China’s Uber) works with an English interface; great for city commutes.
- **Metro**: Beijing, Xi’an, and Shanghai have extensive, cheap subways. Shanghai’s Maglev is a thrill.
- **High-Speed Trains**: For intercity travel, trains rival planes in comfort and punctuality – not needed on this trip, but keep in mind for future.

### Packing & Health
- **Comfortable walking shoes** are non-negotiable – you’ll walk miles daily.
- **Seasonal notes**: Your itinerary implies a blend of seasons (Spring Festival is winter, film/music festivals often spring/autumn). Check forecast: Beijing and Xi’an can be cold (below freezing) from Nov–Feb, while Shanghai is milder. Layer up, bring a cozy scarf/gloves.
- **Air quality**: Download an AQI app. Pack N95 masks if sensitive; they’re also available locally.
- **Travel insurance**: Strongly recommended, covering medical and trip interruption.

### Cultural Etiquette
- **Queuing** is… flexible; assertiveness with patience works best.
- **Tipping** is not customary but appreciated in high-end hotels/restaurants (check if service charge is included).
- **Photography**: Ask before snapping portraits, and avoid military zones.
